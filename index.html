About Me

Hello! I am a PhD Bioinformatics student at UCLA. I became a computational biologist after a stint as a molecular biologist in the biotech start-up world (e.g., 10X Genomics), followed by statistical training (MS Biostatistics; University of Colorado at Anschutz data science consulting core).

These past lives have converged in my work in the Luo Lab (PI: Chongyuan Luo), where our group develops multiomic single-cell and spatial assays. My dissertation work extends machine learning and statistical genetics to these new tools, particularly focusing on how genetic variation and 5mC-methylation interact with aging, the developmental origins of disease, and neuropsychiatric phenotypes.

Learning about other folks’ research is my favorite part of being in academia. Please reach out if you’d like to collaborate or grab a coffee!

Teaching

I am privileged to mentor many talented undergraduate researchers & TA the senior capstone research communication course (UCLA C&S BIO M187). I am currently exploring ways to contribute to pedagology research, with specific interest in teaching strategies for science communication and bioinformatics competency in the transformative age of AI/LLMs.
